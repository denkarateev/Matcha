# MATCHA Bali MVP Go-To-Market

## Executive Summary

MATCHA launches in Bali as a closed-network-style but open-registration marketplace for creator-to-business collaborations. The core promise is simple: verified bloggers/influencers get free access, businesses pay to discover, match, and activate collabs faster than via DMs, agencies, or cold outreach.

The GTM should lean into three truths from the product spec:

- Bali is the only geography for MVP.
- Bloggers are free, businesses pay.
- Trust comes from verification, not platform hype.

The brand should feel local, warm, premium, and playful. The matcha metaphor is a differentiator, not decoration: it should shape copy, creator kits, launch events, and referral language.

## Positioning

### Positioning Statement

MATCHA is the Bali-first collaboration network where verified creators and local businesses match, chat, and launch deals without commissions, agency friction, or endless Instagram DM chasing.

### Category

Creator-business networking marketplace for local commerce.

### Core Differentiation

- Free for verified bloggers.
- Paid subscriptions for businesses only.
- Bumble-style matching plus Offers marketplace.
- Verification-first trust model.
- Built for Bali’s dense, tourism-driven local economy.

### Why It Wins in Bali

- Bali has unusually high creator density and high SMB dependence on content and foot traffic.
- Businesses already pay for discovery through ads, influencers, and agencies, but the workflow is fragmented.
- MATCHA compresses discovery, qualification, and activation into one loop.

### Messaging Pillars

1. Brew connections, blend success.
2. Verified creators, real businesses.
3. Find collabs faster than DMs.
4. No commission, no agency markup.
5. Built for Bali, ready for real deals.

## ICP

### Primary ICP: Businesses

Local businesses in Bali that benefit from creator-led discovery and repeatable content:

- Cafes, restaurants, beach clubs, bars, and nightlife venues.
- Hotels, villas, hostels, and guesthouses.
- Spas, yoga studios, gyms, and wellness brands.
- Surf schools, dive operators, tours, and adventure operators.
- Boutiques, retail shops, and lifestyle brands.

**Decision maker:** owner, general manager, marketing manager, or social media manager.

**Pain points:**

- Reliance on cold DMs and inconsistent creator outreach.
- Difficulty identifying creators with the right audience.
- Slow turnaround from inquiry to post or visit.
- Unclear ROI from influencer collaborations.

**Buying trigger:**

- Need for bookings, foot traffic, reservations, or content volume within days, not weeks.

### Secondary ICP: Creators

Nano to macro bloggers/influencers based in or regularly posting from Bali.

**Segments:**

- Travel creators.
- Food and beverage creators.
- Wellness and fitness creators.
- Beauty and lifestyle creators.
- Nightlife and events creators.

**Pain points:**

- Too many vague inbound offers in DMs.
- Hard to find reliable businesses with real collab intent.
- Need for consistent free or paid opportunities.
- Low trust in random outreach.

**Why they join:**

- Free access.
- More structured collab discovery.
- Better signal from verified business profiles.
- Faster matching and clearer next steps.

## Launch Channels

### Channel Mix

Use channels that match Bali’s real acquisition behavior, not abstract startup playbooks.

1. **Direct outbound to businesses**
   - Walk-in or call outreach to venues in high-density districts.
   - Personal DM/email follow-up with a short demo and verification CTA.
   - Best for first 100 business accounts.

2. **Creator seeding**
   - Invite a small group of recognizable Bali creators to join first.
   - Give them priority onboarding support and early visibility.
   - Their presence makes the feed feel alive and credible.

3. **Local community partnerships**
   - Coworking spaces, hospitality communities, surf communities, wellness studios, and creator groups.
   - Use meetup invitations and partner intros to build trust.

4. **Instagram-first launch content**
   - Reels, Stories, and carousels with the matcha metaphor.
   - Show how a match turns into a booking, visit, or content collab.

5. **WhatsApp distribution**
   - For business owners and local communities, WhatsApp is a practical conversion channel.
   - Use concise onboarding messages and demo links.

6. **On-ground activation**
   - Small launch events, creator breakfasts, and venue walkthroughs.
   - Ideal for seed credibility and screenshots/UGC.

7. **Micro-PR**
   - Local startup, creator economy, and Bali business pages.
   - Focus on “Bali’s new collaboration network” instead of generic app launch copy.

### Channel Priorities by Phase

- Phase 1: direct outbound, creator seeding, WhatsApp, on-ground activation.
- Phase 2: Instagram content, local community partnerships, micro-PR.
- Phase 3: referral loop and paid retargeting only if organic conversion proves repeatable.

## Referral Loop

### Loop Design

The referral loop should feel native to the product, not bolted on.

1. A user finishes a good match, deal, or collaboration.
2. MATCHA generates a share card: “Brewed with MATCHA ☕”.
3. The user shares to Stories, DMs, or WhatsApp.
4. The invitee signs up and completes verification.
5. Both sides receive reward credit.
6. The new user is more likely to create a match or offer, restarting the loop.

### Reward Logic

Use rewards that reinforce actual product usage, not vanity metrics.

- Creator invites creator: both get SuperSwipes.
- Creator invites business: creator gets SuperSwipes.
- Business invites business: both get 1 week of Pro.
- Business invites creator: business gets SuperSwipes.

### Referral Principles

- Reward only after verification to avoid spam.
- Keep the reward visible but not too large; it should accelerate action, not attract low-intent signups.
- Tie the share card to real success moments, not generic invites.

## Creator Acquisition

### Goal

Seed enough verified creators in Bali so businesses see active inventory in the feed from day one.

### Acquisition Strategy

1. **Identify anchor creators**
   - Prioritize creators with credible Bali content, not just follower count.
   - Mix of nano, micro, and mid-tier accounts.

2. **Sell the creator value prop**
   - Free access.
   - More collab opportunities.
   - Structured offers instead of messy DMs.
   - Faster activation once verified.

3. **Use proof, not promises**
   - Show the app feed, match flow, and Offers marketplace.
   - Explain the verification system and why it improves match quality.

4. **Offer white-glove onboarding**
   - Fast manual help to complete the profile.
   - Help creators connect Instagram and pass verification quickly.

5. **Activate creator ambassadors**
   - Give a small group extra visibility and a direct line to the team.
   - Ask for early feedback, testimonials, and social posts.

### Creator Messaging

- “Free access for verified creators.”
- “Find real collabs in Bali without DM chaos.”
- “Your profile is your storefront.”
- “Matches, offers, and deals in one place.”

### Creator Activation Tactics

- Creator breakfasts or coffee meetups in Bali.
- Invite-only demo sessions for trusted creators, even though sign-up remains open.
- Early testimonial snippets for social proof.

## Business Acquisition

### Goal

Convert local businesses into paid subscribers by showing immediate utility: more visibility, more leads, and faster collab activation.

### Acquisition Strategy

1. **Target the highest-fit venues first**
   - Places already investing in social content or promotions.
   - Businesses with visually strong products and repeatable creator need.

2. **Lead with business outcomes**
   - More creator discovery.
   - Faster outreach and responses.
   - Controlled visibility into interested creators.
   - Ability to post offers when they need traffic quickly.

3. **Use the 7-day Pro trial as the hook**
   - Verification unlocks immediate premium utility.
   - The trial should demonstrate value before asking for payment.

4. **Sell to owner/operator reality**
   - Keep the pitch short.
   - Explain that MATCHA reduces agency dependence and outbound chasing.
   - Emphasize no commission on deals.

5. **Create a simple sales motion**
   - Prospect list.
   - Intro message.
   - 10-minute demo.
   - Verification help.
   - Trial activation.
   - Follow-up before expiry.

### Business Messaging

- “Find verified creators in Bali faster.”
- “No commission. No agency markup.”
- “Turn discovery into actual collabs.”
- “Your 7-day Pro trial starts after verification.”

### Business Acquisition Tactics

- Partner with hospitality, wellness, and F&B communities.
- Visit clusters in person around high-density areas.
- Use local WhatsApp groups and owner networks.
- Offer a simple “first offer” setup for businesses that need help getting started.

## 30-Day Rollout

### Week 1: Build Credibility

- Finalize launch messaging and visual system.
- Prepare creator and business pitch decks.
- Recruit a small anchor list of creators and businesses.
- Set up outbound list and tracking sheet.
- Produce launch assets for Instagram, WhatsApp, and on-ground use.

**Exit criteria:**

- Core messaging approved.
- First seed cohort identified.
- Outreach sequences ready.

### Week 2: Seed Supply

- Onboard the first wave of creators.
- Onboard the first wave of businesses.
- Help both sides complete verification.
- Run 1-2 small in-person activation sessions.
- Collect early testimonials and screenshots.

**Exit criteria:**

- Feed has enough density to feel alive.
- First verified profiles visible.
- First matches or offers generated.

### Week 3: Public Launch

- Publish launch content across Instagram, WhatsApp, and community channels.
- Announce MATCHA as Bali’s creator-business collaboration network.
- Push referral sharing after first meaningful match/deal moments.
- Start business follow-up to convert trial users.

**Exit criteria:**

- Organic signups begin to appear without direct outreach.
- At least one repeatable acquisition channel is working.

### Week 4: Optimize and Convert

- Review conversion drop-off by segment.
- Tighten outbound copy and onboarding.
- Double down on the best district/category combinations.
- Run the first experiment backlog.
- Convert trial businesses before expiry.

**Exit criteria:**

- Clear CAC/activation signals.
- Repeatable acquisition motion for both sides.
- Go/no-go evidence for scale-up.

- Share rate after successful match/deal.

These are launch targets, not mature-growth benchmarks:

- 100-200 verified creators in the first 30 days.
- 40-80 verified businesses in the first 30 days.
- 30%+ creator verification rate from signups.
- 40%+ business verification rate from signups.
- 20%+ of verified businesses creating at least one offer or active collab action.
- 10%+ of launches resulting in a shareable referral action.

## Experiments

### Experiment 1: Creator Value Prop

- **Hypothesis:** Creators convert faster when the message is “find real collabs in Bali” instead of “join a networking app.”
- **Test:** Compare two outreach scripts.
- **Success metric:** higher signup-to-verification rate.

### Experiment 2: Business Trial Hook

- **Hypothesis:** Emphasizing the 7-day Pro trial after verification improves business activation.
- **Test:** Trial-first vs feature-first landing copy.
- **Success metric:** verification completion and first action rate.

### Experiment 3: Category-First Acquisition

- **Hypothesis:** Focusing on one or two categories at a time improves feed density and perceived value.
- **Test:** Run district/category-specific campaigns.
- **Success metric:** matches per active user and first-week retention.

### Experiment 4: In-Person vs DM Outreach

- **Hypothesis:** In-person venue visits outperform cold DMs for owner conversion.
- **Test:** Track conversion by channel.
- **Success metric:** verified businesses per 20 contacts.

### Experiment 5: Referral Reward Type

- **Hypothesis:** Utility rewards beat generic gamification.
- **Test:** SuperSwipes vs Pro-week vs mixed rewards.
- **Success metric:** referral completion and post-referral activation.

### Experiment 6: Creator Social Proof

- **Hypothesis:** Testimonials from known Bali creators improve business trust.
- **Test:** Outreach with and without creator quotes/screenshots.
- **Success metric:** reply rate and demo booking rate.

## Operating Rules

- Keep everything Bali-specific for MVP.
- Keep the tone warm, premium, and slightly playful.
- Do not overcomplicate onboarding language.
- Do not position MATCHA like an agency.
- Do not push referrals before product value is felt.
- Do not expand geography before the Bali loop is working.

## Final Recommendation

For launch, prioritize supply density over broad awareness. MATCHA wins when the first users open the app and see credible creators, credible businesses, and a clear reason to act now. The fastest path is: seed creators, sign up businesses, verify quickly, trigger first matches, then let the referral loop and share cards do the storytelling.
